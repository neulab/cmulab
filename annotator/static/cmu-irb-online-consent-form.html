Online Consent

This tool is being developed as part of a research studies conducted by Graham Neubig at Carnegie Mellon University and Antonios Anastasopoulos at George Mason University, funded by the National Science Foundation and National Endowment for the Humanities.

Summary

This tool is developed with the goal of making linguistic documentation easier by (1) allowing you to automatically create a “rough draft” for repetitive tasks such as phoneme recognition, glossing, or morphological analysis of your collected task, and (2) allowing you to more effectively browse the data.

Purpose

The reason why we are providing this tool is to speed your linguistic documentation process, and also to do research to examine the best way to provide aid to linguists who are attempting to document endangered languages. This includes the development of new ways to automatically analyze low-resource or endangered languages, and how to present the results to linguists in a way that will make their documentation more efficient.

Procedures

Once you sign up for the online interface (or install a plugin for your existing interface), you will be able to upload data that you have already annotated or want to annotate. Then you can use your existing data to “train” a linguistic analyzer that will then attempt to analyze new sentences in your language.


With your consent (see below for options), we will collect statistics to analyze how well the tool is doing. This includes the accuracy of the automatic analyses, how much faster the documentation is with the tool than without the tool, and the consistency of the annotation.


We will also occasionally take surveys, which are entirely voluntary and will be aimed at understanding how to best improve the tool. We expect these surveys to take 5-10 minutes.


Participant Requirements

Participation in this study is limited to individuals age 18 and older.


Importantly, in order to upload data to the interface, you must ensure that you have the permission to do so. In other words, you must either own the copyright or have permission from the copyright holder, and usage of data in this way must be allowed by any appropriate organizations (such as an IRB that approved the collection of the data). Participants will be asked to provide consent forms, IRB approvals, and any other relevant documentation that covered the original collection of the data. This documentation must demonstrate that the data was collected with permission to share it with other researchers, or publicly, pursuant with the privacy settings chosen below.


Risks

The main risk associated with this study would be the accidental divulgence of private information. To guard against this risk, we will take a number of security precautions, including:

  1. Standard internet security practices such as use of encrypted communication and user authentication.

  2. The provision of fine-grained privacy settings for your uploaded data, ranging anywhere from making the data entirely public for other users to examine, to completely private where the developers of the tool will not use aggregate statistics of your usage patterns (see below).


Benefits

The main benefit of using the tool will be that it may speed your linguistic annotation process, improve the consistency of your annotation, and potentially allow you to discover new linguistic insights that would not have been possible otherwise.


Compensation & Costs

There is no compensation for your usage of the tool, and there will be no cost to you; use of the interface is free and entirely voluntary.


Future Use of Information

In the future, once we have removed all identifiable information from your data (such as survey responses), we may use the data for our future research studies, or we may distribute the data to other researchers for their research studies. We would do this without getting additional informed consent from you (or your legally authorized representative). Sharing of data with other researchers will only be done in such a manner that you will not be identified, and in a manner consistent with your responses below.
